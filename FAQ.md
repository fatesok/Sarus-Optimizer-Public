# FAQ

## Is the Sarus-Optimizer source code public?

No. This public repository contains public-facing documentation, roadmap material, use cases, case narratives, and benchmark-summary structure. Source code remains private.

## Why publicize the project before releasing code?

The purpose is to build public understanding and credibility through the planning problem, use cases, methodology, and benchmark-summary approach before exposing any implementation details.

## What does Sarus-Optimizer optimize?

Sarus-Optimizer is designed for planning problems where location, capacity, demand, access, cost, and long-term value interact. Initial public focus areas include stadium planning, airport-transit hub planning, candidate-site comparison, transport-constrained facility sizing, and sensitivity reporting.

## Is this an open-source project?

No open-source license is granted in this public repository. The repository is public for communication and documentation purposes only.

## Can the public documents be reused?

Not without permission. Unless a separate written license is granted, the public materials are all rights reserved.

## Are the benchmark results final?

No final numerical benchmark claims are currently published here. Benchmark summaries should be published only after assumptions, outputs, and limitations have been reviewed.

## What makes Sarus-Optimizer different from a simple spreadsheet model?

The public vision is to connect spatial demand, candidate location, feasible capacity, transport constraints, revenue, cost, service level, and sensitivity into one explainable planning framework.

## What kinds of users is this for?

Potential users include facility planners, infrastructure analysts, municipalities, public agencies, investors, consultants, stadium operators, airport planners, transit agencies, and researchers.

## Does Sarus-Optimizer replace engineering or official planning approval?

No. It is intended to support early-stage and mid-stage planning evidence. It does not replace final architectural design, structural engineering, detailed traffic microsimulation, certified appraisal, land assembly work, agency operations planning, or formal approvals.

## How can someone follow progress?

Visitors can star or watch this public repository, follow public updates from Sarus Tesis Planlama, or review the roadmap, milestones, case pages, and benchmark-summary pages as they evolve.
